Study Visa

Study visa is exactly that, it’s a visa that lets you stay in a country under the intention that you are there to study as a student, though study visa requirements can differ depending on the country you want to study in.

  • In the UK study visas are known as a Student Visa. It is for students aged 16 and above. You must first be offered a place on a course by a licensed student sponsor and have a sufficient amount of funds saved up to help support yourself in day to day necessities and housing, if you need financial advice please feel free to contact us.
  • In the United States study visas known as Student Visa (F or M) are required for the sole purpose of studying, any other visa type will not allow you to study there. It is important to have the proper documentation when applying for a Visa, if you need any help understanding the process please contact us.
  • In Canada a study permit is required of all international students of all kinds, to apply for this permit in order to be able to study there, you must first answer some questions to assess if you are eligible or not. Then once approved you must apply for a Visitor visa or Electronic travel authorisation (eTA) to enter the country. A study permit is not a Visa. For more clarification on what you need to prepare and steps to take, please contact us.

Work Permit/Visa

Depending on the country you will be required to have a work permit or work visa in order to start legally working and earning in a different country.

  • In the UK there are many types of specific work visas you can get like long/short term visas, Investor-business development and talent visas, other work visas and sponsorship visas. More information can be found on the countries government website, however we can help guide you on which one you should get. So please contact us and ask advice from our team, we are here to help.
  • A work visa in the United States can be a little tricky to navigate your way through but essentially you can obtain a temporary non-immigrant visa for the duration of your employment contract and exchange visitor visa for certain approved programs. For more information contact our team.
  • To work in Canada you must obtain a work permit, depending on the circumstances the type of work permits can vary from temporary to permanent depending on the duration of your employment contract. We can help you find the right one for your travel needs, please contact a member of the team to find out more information.

Tourist Visa

If you're thinking about studying in a different country, getting a tourist visa to take a trip to see what it’s like before you commit to studying there, is a great idea. You can see the sites and have fun with leisure activities, while you think about moving there.

  • In the UK depending on what country you come from you may not need a visa for tourism/holiday purposes, if you do then you can get a visa waiver. Otherwise you may need to apply for a visitor visa instead. For more information depending on your needs please discuss with a member of our staff to advise you on the best route possible.
  • In the US, unless you're applying for a visa waiver program, you can obtain a (B-2) tourist visitor visa for nonimmigrant temporary personal travel. If you need more information and advice on what to prepare, please get in touch with us by going to our contact page.
  • A tourist visa to Canada is the same as applying for a visitor visa and can differ depending on the supporting documents you need to prepare depending on the purpose of your visit. To find out if you're eligible or not contact us, so we can find out together.

For more detailed information, check out these helpful links:

Visitor Visa

This type of visa is for non-immigrants that travel to different countries for temporary business, tourist and study purposes. This is mostly dependent on the country's regulation on the reason for entry.

  • In the UK a visitor visa is known as a standard visitor visa for business, tourism and study purposes. Usually you can stay up to 6months but depending on the circumstances you could stay longer. You must ensure that you check if you're eligible or not before applying. IF you have any questions please get in touch with us.
  • Visitor visas for the US are classified as non immigrant visas. Depending on the reason for entry it can be further categorised, as such: temporary stay for business (B-1), tourism (B-2), or for both combined is (B-1/B-2). For more information and guidance please contact us.
  • To visit Canada is essentially the same as applying for a tourist visa, for both purposes they are classed as a Visitor visa and more information on what you need to prepare will depend on the reason for your visit. Contact us for more information on what you should prepare and guidance on how to apply.

Internship Visa

Internships at Overseas Placements will often require a work permit/visa to be allowed access into the country for duration of the contracted employment internship start and finish dates. You can also get a student visa for internship opportunities but will not be able to find work, so it is important to correctly identify what kind of visa is required depending on the country's regulations.

  • To apply for an internship in the UK, you will need a ‘Temporary work-International agreement visa’, which has replaced the ‘Temporary worker government-authorised exchange Visa (T5)’. You can apply for this visa 3 before your start date. For more information and instructions please contact us.
  • For an internship in the US you will need a ‘J-1 exchange visitor visa’ for any traineeship or internship program participation in the USA. contact us for more advice and guidance in obtaining an internship visa.
  • To gain an internship visa for Canada you are required to be part of an approved list of countries eligible, 18-30 years of age and depending on the type of international experience you will need to apply for a specific permit to enter. Typically for an international Co-op (internship), you must get an employer specific work permit before being granted entry. If you need more information and clarification on what you need to prepare, please feel free to contact us.

For more detailed information, check out these helpful links:

Business Visa

Applying for a business visa means that you would like to enter a country under the presumption that your intention is to temporarily stay to attend business functions and purposes.

  • To get a business visa for entry into the UK, is usually a standard visitor visa under the category of ‘Investor-business development and talent visas’ section. If you're a new start-up entrepreneur, who wants to set up business in the UK then it’s recommended you get an innovator or start-up visa.
  • To apply for a business visa in the US, you need to check whether you're in a participating country because if you are you may not even need a visa to enter. Though if you do, then you most likely need a visitor visa (B-1) to travel for temporary business matters. For more advice, please contact our team.
  • To gain a business visa for entry to Canada you would apply for a visitor visa with an electronic travel authorisation (eTA) and valid passport, as well as state that you are staying for less than 6 month. For more information on eligibility please contact us to make sure you are applying for the correct visa.
